Overview

Generative AI represents one of the most transformative developments in artificial intelligence, enabling machines to create human-like text, images, code, and multimedia content. This section covers the fundamental architectures, training methodologies, and applications that define the current state of generative AI.

Attention Mechanisms

The foundation of modern AI, attention mechanisms allow models to focus on relevant parts of input sequences, enabling long-range dependencies and parallel processing.

$$\text{Attention}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$$

Neural Language Models

From RNNs to Transformers, language models predict the probability distribution over sequences of tokens, enabling text generation and understanding.

$$P(x_1, ..., x_T) = \prod_{t=1}^T P(x_t | x_1, ..., x_{t-1})$$

Diffusion Processes

Diffusion models generate high-quality images by learning to reverse a noise corruption process, creating stunning visual content.

$$q(x_t|x_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}(x_t; \sqrt{1-\beta_t}x_{t-1}, \beta_t I)$$

Emergent Capabilities

Large-scale models exhibit emergent behaviors like few-shot learning, reasoning, and cross-modal understanding that weren't explicitly programmed.

$$\text{Capability} \propto \text{Scale}^{\alpha} \cdot \text{Data}^{\beta}$$
